Retailer Rating Criteria

Each retailer is given a final ranking depending on the combination of their Green, Price and Trust ratings

Green Rating

A synthesis of all green programs and offers that the retailer provides while also considering their policy towards fossil fuels. A few key criteria considered are:

  • Offers GreenPower to customers
  • The retailer is carbon neutral or provides a carbon-neutral option
  • Invests in renewable energy
  • Does not support fossil fuels (including parent or sister companies)

Price Rating

A mostly quantitative analysis of the pricing offered by retailers for both single rate and time of use plans. Five coins indicates best pricing (cheapest), one coin means worse pricing (expensive). A few key criteria considered are:

  • Pricing of plans offered from the retailers Energy Facts Sheets
  • The price ranking of retailers completed by the Victorian Essential Services Commission
  • The discount percentage in relation to the Victorian Default Offer
  • Additional costs and fees

Trust Rating

An averaging of each companies current customer satisfaction, complied through online reviews. Also any compliance action made by the Victorian Essential Services Commission. A few key criteria include:

  • Aggregation of online reviews by customers about the retailer
  • Fines or compliance action taken against the retailer
  • Percentage of customer base that complain about the service (where available)

Top three greenest and best priced energy retailers in Melbourne

Diamond Energy

Diamond Energy is by far the greenest energy retailer in Victoria with large investments into renewable energy. They actively invest in renewable energy projects all across Australia. Their pricing and overall brand trust is solid and you can be reassured that if you use this retailer you are investing in a sustainable green future.

Green Rating: 5/5

  • When a customer uses one megawatt of energy they are able to sell 187% more renewable energy into the grid
  • Supports and manages over thirty renewable energy facilities across Australia
  • Has invested in community solar projects, sometimes free of charge
  • The highest battery feed in tariff of any retailer in Victoria
  • Slightly above average solar feed-in tariff

Price Rating: 4/5

  • Competitive pricing for all levels of energy consumption
  • Both their time of use and all-day charges are reasonable relative to the competition
  • Their GreenPower pricing is slightly more expensive, as is their daily charge

Trust Rating: 4.5/5

  • Has an average of 4 stars from customer reviews
  • A low level of complaints from their customer base

Energy Locals

Energy Locals is a carbon neutral retailer with affordable pricing. If you are renting or don't have solar then their plans are a great option, particularly if you want to buy GreenPower to further offset your carbon emissions. Even though they charge a monthly subscription their pricing is still more affordable than most year on year.

Green Rating: 4/5

  • Offers peer to peer energy trading through partnerships
  • Supports some community renewable energy projects
  • Is publicly opposed to fossil fuel energy investments
  • Slightly above average solar feed-in tariff

Price Rating: 4/5

  • Charges a monthly subscription fee regardless of your energy use
  • Both their time of use and all-day charges are reasonable
  • Has a cheap GreenPower price relative to the competition

Trust Rating: 4/5

  • On average customer reviews are near 4.2 stars and complaints are low but a few
  • Was part of a $52,000 repayment compliance action for not offering the best pricing messaging to some customers

Discover Energy

Has a strong emphasis on person-to-person energy trading through its tech platform. Offers strong incentives for going solar, buying an EV, and having a large in-home battery, although the battery tariff is lower than Diamond Energy. If you have a strong emphasis on trust and communication then this is the retailer for you, also you can use their sister company to buy and install solar.

Green Rating: 4/5

  • Offers peer to peer energy trading through their own software
  • Has an electric vehicle program and a battery tariff
  • A strong proponent of local renewable energy sharing
  • Is sister companies to the solar installation company One Stop Warehouse
  • Slightly above average solar feed-in tariff

Price Rating: 2.5/5

  • Their pricing can get expensive for certain homes and levels of energy usage
  • Both their time of use and all-day charges can get expensive for some
  • Has a more expensive GreenPower price than other retailers

Trust Rating: 5/5

  • Has the best customer reviews of any retailer on this list, currently at 5 stars
  • Pricing transparency and contact information is easily accessible

Nine retailers that are still a green choice

Amber Electric

A tech-focused retailer that provides customers updates every 30 minutes through their app about energy prices. The ESC ranks Amber Electric as one of the most expensive retailers in Victoria when looking at median pricing.

Green Rating 4.5/5

  • Vehemently opposed to fossil fuel investments and encourages political action for renewable energy
  • All plans are carbon neutral by default
  • Has a program for battery tariffs and is rolling out an EV charging program

Price Rating: 1.5/5

  • Has a monthly subscription cost regardless of energy usage
  • As rated by the ESC, it has one of the most expensive plans in Victoria by median pricing
  • They provide live energy pricing to show when energy is cheapest (also when renewables are producing maximum output), however most consumers use the majority of their energy when renewables are not producing a majority of their energy

Trust Rating: 4/5

  • Has good customer reviews, around an average of 4.2 stars
  • It’s difficult to calculate some of their pricing, in particular, their solar and battery tariffs

Ovo Energy

Not able to review

A UK-based energy company that entered the Australian market in 2019. A solid track record of being green-focused at reasonable price points. With a strong history of offering programs and incentives around batteries, solar, and EVs (in the UK).

Green Rating 4/5

  • Encourages the use of renewable energy for the grid and has previously invested in community solar (UK)
  • All plans are carbon neutral and have a 10% GreenPower tier by default
  • Has an EV charging program (in Victoria)
  • Does not have a battery tariff or demand response program

Price Rating: 3.5/5

  • Offers very affordable pricing for both time of use and all-day pricing
  • They use a complicated direct debit system that averages the customers monthly usage and then charges the energy usage each month against that averaged amount
  • Rated as one of the more cost-efficient retailers in Victoria by the ESC
  • Their GreenPower price is quite expensive relative to the competition

Trust Rating: Not available

  • This company does not have enough online reviews for us to determine its trustworthiness
  • Although their bill smoothing policy is somewhat complicated relative to more straightforward payment methods, overall their pricing and information is easily accessible

How does a solar panel work?

A solar panel consists of solar cells that convert the energy of photons sent by the sun into excited electrons via a semiconductor material, commonly doped silicon. These excited electrons create an electrical current that can be harnessed and converted for use as electricity.

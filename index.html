    What do these ratings mean?

    Green Rating

    A synthesis of all green programs and offers that the retailer provides while also considering their policy towards fossil fuels. A few key criteria considered are:

    • Offers GreenPower to customers
    • The retailer is carbon neutral or provides a carbon-neutral option
    • Invests in renewable energy
    • Does not support fossil fuels (including parent or sister companies)
    • Offers incentives or programs to promote electric vehicles, solar, demand response or community energy

    Affordability

    A mostly quantitative analysis of the pricing offered by retailers for both single rate and time of use plans. Five coins indicates best pricing (cheapest), one coin means worse pricing (expensive). A few key criteria considered are:

    • Pricing of plans offered from the retailers Energy Facts Sheets
    • The price ranking of retailers completed by the Essential Services Commission
    • The discount percentage in relation to the Default Offer
    • Additional costs and fees

    Below is just a sample of a few high quality energy providers in Aus

    Diamond Energy

    Diamond Energy is by far the greenest energy provider in Australia with large investments into renewable energy.

    They actively invest and operate in renewable energy projects all across Australia. Their pricing is reasonable relative to their impact and you can be reassured that you are without a doubt investing in the greenest energy provider in Australia. Diamond Energy is also the best choice for battery owners with a huge battery feed in tariff.

    Energy Locals

    Energy Locals is a carbon neutral retailer with great affordable pricing that invests into the community, they do however charge a subscription fee of $15 a month.

    If you are renting or don't have solar then their plans are a great option, particularly if you want to buy GreenPower to further offset your carbon emissions. Even though they charge a monthly subscription their pricing is still more affordable than most year on year.

    Enova Energy

    Only available in NSW and QLD. Enova Energy purchases and invests in a significant of amount of renewable energy, but can get expensive.

    They also heavily reinvest into their not-for-profit arm for community projects and renewables. Enova Energy offers strong incentives for going solar, buying an EV, and having a large in-home battery.

    Momentum Energy

    Owned by the largest renewable generator in Aus, Hydro Tasmania. Its unfortunate they don't offer carbon neutral plans.

    On the one hand you would be supporting active operation of renewable facilities in Tassie, on the other hand they also own fossil gas facilities, don't offer very competitive pricing, and charge a lot for GreenPower energy.

    PowerShop

    Sold out to Royal Dutch Shell but they are still offering carbon neutral plans and at pretty affordable rates.

    When they were bought out they sold all of their renewable power stations to another company but they still buy back a lot of that same energy. Overall still pretty green and affordable despite the buyout by Shell.

    Avoid the dirtiest energy providers in Australia and save your money

    Origin Energy

    Origin is the largest energy provider in Australia and is also one of the largest polluters in the whole country, currently 4th.

    They are the largest user of natural gas as a fossil fuel for energy production and they still operate coal fired plants. If you had your heart set on using this provider we would recommend at least using their carbon neutral plan.

    AGL Energy

    AGL is Australia's largest energy producer and continually wins the title of biggest polluter in the country.

    AGL has and continues to operate with significant investments in both coal and natural gas. AGL does offer carbon neutral plans customers can opt into but putting lipstick on a lump of coal won't make it any prettier. They have also been hit with a high number of compliance actions and fines over the last few years.

    Energy Australia

    The third largest energy retailer in the country with large investments into coal fired power and natural gas all over the world.

    A subsidiary of the Hong Kong CLP Group. You can read about all of Energy Australia's coal fired power plants right on their website, so kind of them right?

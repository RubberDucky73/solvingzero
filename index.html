    What do these ratings mean?

    Green Rating

    A synthesis of all green programs and offers that the retailer provides while also considering their policy towards fossil fuels. A few key criteria considered are:

    • Offers GreenPower to customers
    • The retailer is carbon neutral or provides a carbon-neutral option
    • Invests in renewable energy
    • Does not support fossil fuels (including parent or sister companies)
    • Offers incentives or programs to promote electric vehicles, solar, demand response or community energy

    Affordability

    A mostly quantitative analysis of the pricing offered by retailers for both single rate and time of use plans. Five coins indicates best pricing (cheapest), one coin means worse pricing (expensive). A few key criteria considered are:

    • Pricing of plans offered from the retailers Energy Facts Sheets
    • The price ranking of retailers completed by the Essential Services Commission
    • The discount percentage in relation to the Default Offer
    • Additional costs and fees

    The best options that you should consider based on your criteria Learn more about the ratings

    Diamond Energy

    Based in Melbourne Diamond Energy is by far the greenest energy retailer in Australia with large investments into renewable energy. They actively invest in renewable energy projects all across Australia. Their pricing and overall brand trust is solid and you can be reassured that if you use this retailer you are investing in a sustainable green future. This is also the best choice for battery owners.

    Energy Locals

    Energy Locals is a carbon neutral retailer with affordable pricing. If you are renting or don't have solar then their plans are a great option, particularly if you want to buy GreenPower to further offset your carbon emissions. Even though they charge a monthly subscription their pricing is still more affordable than most year on year.

    Enova Energy

    Only available in NSW and QLD. Enova Energy purchases and invests in a significant of amount of renewable energy. Also heavily reinvests into their not-for-profit arm for community projects and renewables. Offers strong incentives for going solar, buying an EV, and having a large in-home battery. If you have a strong emphasis on trust and communication then this is the retailer for you, also you can use their sister company to buy and install solar.

    ReAmped

    Rated by the ESC as one of the most affordable retailers in VIC by median pricing. They are carbon neutral and they offer some of the best pricing for GreenPower and solar tariffs, however they do charge more for grid power if you go on with their special solar plan. This is still the best option for solar owners.

    Amber Electric

    A tech-focused retailer that provides customers updates every 30 minutes through their app about energy prices. The ESC ranks Amber Electric as one of the most expensive retailers when looking at median pricing.

    Avoid the dirtiest energy providers in Australia and save your money

    Origin Energy

    Origin is the largest retailer in Australia and is also one of the largest polluters in the whole country. They are the largest user of natural gas as a fossil fuel for energy production in Australia. They also use coal fired plants for energy production. If you choose this retailer we would recommend opting into the carbon neutral plan at the very least.

    AGL Energy

    AGL is Australia's largest energy producer and also its largest carbon emitter by a huge margin, with significant investments in both coal and natural gas powered production. They have also been hit with a high number of compliance actions and fines over the last few years. They do offer carbon neutral plans that customers can opt into.

    Energy Australia

    The third largest energy retailer in the country with large investments into coal fired power and natural gas. A subsidiary of the Hong Kong CLP Group. You can read about all of Energy Australia's coal fired power plants right on their website. If you choose this retailer we would recommend opting into the carbon neutral plan at the very least.

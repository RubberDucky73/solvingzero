Retailer Rating Criteria

Each retailer is given a final rank determined by a combination of their green rating, affordability, and trustworthiness

Green Rating

A synthesis of all green programs and offers that the retailer provides while also considering their policy towards fossil fuels. A few key criteria considered are:

  • Offers GreenPower to customers
  • The retailer is carbon neutral or provides a carbon-neutral option
  • Invests in renewable energy
  • Does not support fossil fuels (including parent or sister companies)
  • Offers incentives or programs to promote electric vehicles, solar, demand response or community energy

Affordability

A mostly quantitative analysis of the pricing offered by retailers for both single rate and time of use plans. Five coins indicates best pricing (cheapest), one coin means worse pricing (expensive). A few key criteria considered are:

  • Pricing of plans offered from the retailers Energy Facts Sheets
  • The price ranking of retailers completed by the Victorian Essential Services Commission
  • The discount percentage in relation to the Victorian Default Offer
  • Additional costs and fees

Trustworthiness

An averaging of each companies current customer satisfaction, complied through online reviews. Also any compliance action made by the Victorian Essential Services Commission in the last few years. A few key criteria include:

  • Aggregation of online reviews by customers about the retailer
  • Fines or compliance action taken against the retailer
  • Percentage of customer base that complain about the service (where available)

Top three greenest and affordable energy retailers in Melbourne

Diamond Energy

Best for: Supporting green energy in Australia

Diamond Energy is by far the greenest energy retailer in Victoria with large investments into renewable energy. They actively invest in renewable energy projects all across Australia. Their pricing and overall brand trust is solid and you can be reassured that if you use this retailer you are investing in a sustainable green future. This is also the best choice for battery owners.

Green Rating: 5/5

  • When a customer uses one megawatt of energy they are able to sell 187% more renewable energy into the grid
  • Supports and manages over thirty renewable energy facilities across Australia
  • Has invested in community solar projects, sometimes free of charge
  • The highest battery feed in tariff of any retailer in Victoria
  • Slightly above average solar feed-in tariff

Affordability: 4/5

  • Competitive pricing for all levels of energy consumption
  • Both their time of use and all-day charges are reasonable relative to the competition
  • Their GreenPower pricing is slightly more expensive, as is their daily charge

Trustworthiness: 4.5/5

  • Has an average of 4 stars from customer reviews
  • A low level of complaints from their customer base

Energy Locals

Best for: You want to buy cheap GreenPower

Energy Locals is a carbon neutral retailer with affordable pricing. If you are renting or don't have solar then their plans are a great option, particularly if you want to buy GreenPower to further offset your carbon emissions. Even though they charge a monthly subscription their pricing is still more affordable than most year on year.

Green Rating: 4/5

  • Offers peer to peer energy trading through partnerships
  • Supports some renewable energy projects
  • Is publicly opposed to fossil fuel energy investments
  • Slightly above average solar feed-in tariff

Affordability: 3.5/5

  • Charges a monthly subscription fee regardless of your energy use
  • Both their time of use and all-day charges are reasonable
  • Has a cheap GreenPower price relative to the competition

Trustworthiness: 4/5

  • On average customer reviews are near 4.2 stars
  • Has a low level of complaints from customers but a few
  • Was part of a $52,000 repayment compliance action for not offering the best pricing messaging to some customers

Solving Zero is not affiliated with, partnered with, or owned by any energy retailer. We make no money from you changing retailer. These ratings and opinions are 100% independently sourced.

Discover Energy

Best for: Wanting a low stress trustworthy brand

Has a strong emphasis on person-to-person energy trading through its tech platform. Offers strong incentives for going solar, buying an EV, and having a large in-home battery, although the battery tariff is lower than Diamond Energy. If you have a strong emphasis on trust and communication then this is the retailer for you, also you can use their sister company to buy and install solar.

Green Rating: 4/5

  • Offers peer to peer energy trading through their own software
  • Has an electric vehicle program and a battery tariff
  • A strong proponent of local renewable energy sharing
  • Is sister companies to the solar installation company One Stop Warehouse
  • Slightly above average solar feed-in tariff

Affordability: 2.5/5

  • Their pricing can get expensive for certain homes and levels of energy usage
  • Both their time of use and all-day charges can get expensive for some
  • Has a more expensive GreenPower price than other retailers

Trustworthiness: 5/5

  • Has the best customer reviews of any retailer on this list, currently at 5 stars
  • Pricing transparency and contact information is easily accessible

Three runner-up retailers that are still great choices

ReAmped Energy

Best for: On a budget but want to be green

Rated by the ESC as one of the most affordable retailers in VIC by median pricing. They are carbon neutral and they offer some of the best pricing for GreenPower and solar tariffs, however they do charge more for grid power if you go on with their special solar plan. This is still the best option for solar owners.

Green Rating 3.5/5

  • Carbon neutral energy for all plans
  • Does not have an EV program, battery tariff, or demand response system
  • If you have solar they will charge you more for gird energy - most likely to balance their great solar feed-in tariff. Although their non-solar plan still has an above average solar tariff

Affordability: 4.5/5

  • One of the most affordable retailers in Victoria as rated by the ESC
  • Both the time of use and all-day pricing are affordable and competitive
  • Their GreenPower pricing is one of the most affordable

Trustworthiness: 3.5/5

  • Has an average of 3.2 stars for their customer reviews
  • Pricing and contact information is easily accessible

Amber Electric

Best for: Want a tech approach to your energy

A tech-focused retailer that provides customers updates every 30 minutes through their app about energy prices. The ESC ranks Amber Electric as one of the most expensive retailers in Victoria when looking at median pricing.

Green Rating 4/5

  • Vehemently opposed to fossil fuel investments and encourages political action for renewable energy
  • All plans are carbon neutral by default
  • Has a program for battery tariffs and is rolling out an EV charging program
  • Through their app you can easily identify demand response time periods

Affordability: 1.5/5

  • Has a monthly subscription cost regardless of energy usage
  • As rated by the ESC, it has one of the most expensive plans in Victoria by median pricing
  • They provide live energy pricing to show when energy is cheapest (also when renewables are producing maximum output), however most consumers use the majority of their energy when renewables are not producing a majority of their energy

Trustworthiness: 4/5

  • Has good customer reviews, around an average of 4.2 stars
  • It’s difficult to calculate some of their pricing, in particular, their solar and battery tariffs

Ovo Energy

Not able to review

Best for: Want to pay your bill upfront

A UK-based energy company that entered the Australian market in 2019. A solid track record of being green-focused at reasonable price points although not all programs are currently on offer in Vic. Their pricing uses a averaged monthly bill method that some people could find as a negative or positive.

Green Rating 4/5

  • Encourages the use of renewable energy for the grid and has previously invested in community solar (UK)
  • All plans are carbon neutral and have a 10% GreenPower tier by default
  • Has an EV charging program (in Victoria)
  • Does not have a battery tariff or demand response program

Affordability: 3.5/5

  • Offers very affordable pricing for both time of use and all-day pricing
  • They use a complicated direct debit system that averages the customers monthly usage and then charges the energy usage each month against that averaged amount
  • Rated as one of the more cost-efficient retailers in Victoria by the ESC
  • Their GreenPower price beyond the default 10% is quite expensive relative to the competition

Trustworthiness: Not available

  • This company does not have enough online reviews for us to determine its trustworthiness
  • Although their bill smoothing policy is somewhat complicated relative to more straightforward payment methods, overall their pricing and information is easily accessible

The remaining retailers

CovaU Energy

A solid retailer for affordability and green rating, with an exceptional history of trustworthiness through great customer reviews and support. This retailer would be a good second option if you decide not to go with Discover Energy based on trustworthiness.

Green Rating 3/5

  • Does not offer carbon neutral plans but is a big promoter of its customers utilising GreenPower
  • Has a solar plan with a great feed-in tariff but charges a higher rate for grid power if you use it
  • Does not offer an EV, battery or demand response program

Affordability: 3/5

  • Both the time of use and sing-rate pricing is higher than the competition
  • The ESC rates CovaU as on of the more expensive retailers in Victoria when looking at median pricing
  • Their GreenPower is only slightly more expensive than the competition

Trustworthiness: 4.5/5

  • On average customers have given CovaU a rating of 4.3 stars
  • Complaint from customers are few and far between

PowerShop

In 2021 they were bought out by oil and gas conglomerate Shell, historically one of the most polluting companies in the world. Through this purchase agreement PowerShops renewable wind farms and hydropower stations were sold to another company, ICG. That all being said PowerShop still has some solid green programs paired with affordable pricing.

Green Rating: 3/5

  • Parent company is the one of the largest global polluters in history
  • Has carbon neutral plans on top of their GreenPower offers
  • Does not offer a battery tariff but does buy renewable energy credits from ICG
  • Their solar feed-in tariff is the minimum allowed

Affordability: 4/5

  • Their plan pricing is affordable for both time of use and sign-rate plans
  • Their GreenPower pricing is cheap
  • The ESC ranks them favourably for their plan pricing when calculating the median

Trustworthiness: 3/5

  • They were fined $300,000 for allegedly discriminating renewable energy customers (those with solar rooftop systems) by not offering the same tariffs offered to non-renewable energy customers
  • It is difficult to get an honest customer review average as they have been bombarded with negative reviews since announcing the Shell purchase

Momentum Energy

Owned by parent company Tassie Hydro this retailer has some green programs and offers for customers. Their main advantage is you know your money is going to a parent company that invest heavily into the green electricity grid of Tasmania.

Green Rating 3.5/5

  • Parent company invests heavily and manages renewable energy facilities across Tasmania
  • Does not offer community energy sharing, battery tariffs or EVs
  • Will charge more for basic grid energy if you are on their solar plan
  • Is not carbon neutral

Affordability: 3/5

  • Slightly more expensive than most when examining all levels of energy consumption (single, couples and families)
  • The ESC has ranked them slightly more expensive when looking at median pricing
  • Their GreenPower pricing is more expensive than most of the competition

Trustworthiness: 2/5

  • Their customer reviews have an average of 2.1 stars
  • The amount of complaints filed about this retailer is quite high
  • Was part of a $52,000 repayment compliance action for not offering the best pricing messaging to some customers

Simple Energy

Owned by parent company ENGIE, Simple Energy began offering carbon neutral plans as of 2021. Historically ENGIE was a large investor into coal around the world but has since divested, they are however still heavily investing into natural gas as a fossil fuel.

Green Rating 2.5/5

  • Has carbon neutral plans that you can opt into
  • Only some of their plans offer GreenPower and its hard to tell which is which
  • Has a battery tariff, EV program and a demand response system

Affordability: 3.5/5

  • Resaonbly priced plans for most customers
  • The solar tariff is at the legal minimum
  • The ESC ranks their median pricing at about the middle of the VIC retail market

Trustworthiness: 1/5

  • Customers have rated an average of 1.3 stars
  • The retailer had to pay $2.5 million in penalties for external sales agents’ alleged fraudulent transfer of around 500 energy customers

Red Energy

Owned by parent company Snowy Hydro which is responsible for several hydro power stations. Red Energy supports the use of fossil fuels through its promotion of natural gas usage.

Green Rating 2.5/5

  • Actively uses and supports natural gas as a fossil fuel for energy production
  • Is not carbon neutral for any of its plans
  • Does not offer battery tariffs, community energy sharing or demand response programs
  • Does have an above average solar feed-in tariff

Affordability: 2/5

  • Has one of the most expensive GreenPower prices of any retailer
  • Ranked by the ESC as one of the most expensive energy retailers available when looking at median pricing

Trustworthiness: 2/5

  • Has an average rating of around 2 stars from customers
  • A somewhat sizeable amount of complaints from customers

Elysian

A small Melbourne based retailer that has some solid green programs and offers but is not that affordable nor does it have a high trustworthiness rating.

Green Rating 2/5

  • Does not offer GreenPower on any of its plans
  • Is not carbon neutral
  • Does offer a very generous solar feed-in tarrif

Affordability: 2/5

  • Is ranked by ESC as one of the most expensive retailers in VIC based on median pricing
  • Even at the lowest energy consumption level this retailers prices can be significantly higer than the competition

Trustworthiness: 2/5

  • On average customer reviews are around 2.0 stars for this retailer

How does a solar panel work?

A solar panel consists of solar cells that convert the energy of photons sent by the sun into excited electrons via a semiconductor material, commonly doped silicon. These excited electrons create an electrical current that can be harnessed and converted for use as electricity.
